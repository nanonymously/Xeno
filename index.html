In her own words

When I joined Xeno, marketing wasn't a machine yet — it was a blank page, a broken domain, and a lot of question marks.

So the work this year wasn't only about leads or numbers. It was about quietly building the foundation that would let everything else stand: the systems, the cadence, the trust.

Some of it showed up in dashboards. Most of it didn't. But the part I'll carry isn't the SQLs or the impressions — it's knowing I took something close to nothing and made it real, and watched it begin to grow on its own.

That's the part I'm proud of. And it's only the beginning.

— Nandita
