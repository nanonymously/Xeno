Q3 Marketing Plan (Jul–Sep 2026)

Owner: Nandita Khanna · Reviewing: Q2 2026 (Apr–Jun) · Status: Working plan (living document)

Signal Touch launch is a separate plan. This covers the core marketing engine.

In one line: Fix conversion, not volume — work a focused Top 50 + Next 200, hand sales fewer but higher-quality leads, and run only the activities that have actually produced SQLs, to hit 10 SQLs in Q3 while pre-building the October launch.

The goal

Marketing exists to create meetings that become SQLs. In Q2 we created plenty of engagement but converted very little of it — 82 relevant engagements turned into just 4 meetings and 5 SQLs (goal was 7). Q3 is about fixing that conversion on a clean, focused account list — not sending more.

Q3 target: 10 SQLs.

Operating principles

  1. Focus on the few best-fit accounts, not the whole list — work the Top 50 + Next 200, not all 5,876.
  2. Qualify hard; don't blast — give sales fewer, higher-quality leads to focus on, not mass.
  3. Spend only where it works — every paid activity has an "is this working?" checkpoint; if it isn't producing meetings, we stop.
  4. Prove it or it didn't happen — if we can't attribute and time it, we don't claim it. Close the tracking gap.
  5. Lead with proof and good creative — events, case studies, interactive campaigns; product detail comes after.

How we compete (competitor read)

  • Capillary — enterprise-loyalty heavyweight. Wins on analyst recognition (Forrester Wave / Everest Leader), scale (1.9B members, 415+ brands), acquisitions, big events. We will not out-spend it on analysts or scale.
  • MoEngage / CleverTap — app-first, cross-channel engagement. Win on benchmark/industry reports, branded communities & events (MoEngage #GROWTH), heavy case studies + G2 reviews, and an aggressive AI-agent / decisioning narrative.
  • Where Xeno wins: purpose-built for retail (POS / loyalty / transaction-connected, India + GCC), proven incremental revenue (Taco Bell +69% repeat, Bestseller +40% on digital, 60x ROI), and an autonomous decisioning story — "every retail marketer a manager of agents," not assistive AI.
  • So our playbook copies what works for challengers — retail-specific research, named case studies, intimate founder-led events, a sharp POV — and skips what we can't fund (analyst placements, mass events).

The 5 candidate activities (commit to 3 after the pitch)

All five are grounded in what produced SQLs this year and what the category does well:

  1. Intimate events & roundtables — Top 50; founder-led, retail-CXO rooms. Best SQL conversion all year (~7:1).
  2. Webinar + retail research / benchmark — Rest + feeds all tiers; a "State of AI in Retail Marketing / Loyalty" report powers a thought-leadership-led webinar (not product-led) — it earns attention by sharing a point of view, with product as the follow-up.
  3. ABM + interactive / game campaigns — Top 50 (personalised) + Next 200 (segmented); Xeno's creative edge (Squid Games, Loyalty Dilemma). The SQL workhorse.
  4. Inbound + case-study / proof engine — all tiers; gated case studies (Taco Bell, Bestseller), ROI calculator, website forms. Highest quality per touch (~5:1).
  5. Founder-led LinkedIn + agentic narrative (optional / aspirational) — Top 50 / 200 awareness; Pranav's POV on the decisioning story + Xeno Pulse. Low-cost and differentiated; include only if bandwidth allows.

My lean for the 3: #1 (events), #3 (ABM + game), #4 (inbound) as the SQL engine — with #2 (research + webinar) and #5 (founder LinkedIn) as awareness feeders if budget / bandwidth allow. Final call after the pitch.

Qualifying harder — quality leads over mass

We hand sales fewer, higher-quality leads — not mass — by qualifying harder before the hand-off. Three steps:

1. Define what counts as an MQL. An engagement only qualifies if it clears two gates: - Fit — the account is on the Top 50 / Next 200 ICP list, and the person is a buyer or influencer (CRM, loyalty, marketing, growth, digital, e-commerce, or an IT decision-maker) — not HR, legal, or a junior contact. - Intent — a real signal, not a single open: demo / pricing / contact-form, repeat engagement, webinar or event attendance, or a completed game/quiz.

2. Score and route (simple 2×2).

High intent Low intent
High fit (Top 50/200) Hot → sales follow-up in 24h, book the meeting Warm → tight ABM + event/webinar invite
Low fit (rest) Watch → light nurture; enrich to confirm fit Drop / suppress — out of the active list

Only Hot (high-fit + high-intent) counts toward the MQL number we report and chase.

3. Catch it while warm. Every Hot engagement enters the 24h sales follow-up SLA, with the activity + asset logged — which is also how we finally start measuring activation→SQL time.

What this gives sales: fewer, higher-quality leads to focus on instead of mass. And in any case, every Hot lead carries a 24-hour sales reach-out SLA.

Early signals — what you'll see in the first 30 days

SQLs have a ~4–6 week lead time, so real SQLs land late August. These July proof-points show momentum before the number moves:

  • Top 50 + Next 200 enriched to ≥50% of the minimum-data standard (from ~6%).
  • Tracking live: every Hot engagement logged with activity + asset; activation→SQL clock running.
  • Generic blasts stopped; first interactive campaign + ABM wave 1 shipped.
  • First Hot hand-offs to sales within the 24h SLA.
  • Research fielded + webinar invites out; 1–2 new case studies published.

If these are in place by end-July, the SQLs follow. If they're not, we'll know early — not in September.

Alignment with the org plan (Signal Touch)

Signal Touch — Xeno's AI product line — launches mid-October. This Q3 plan is deliberately its foundation quarter: same tiers, same motions, built so nothing is wasted.

  • Tiers nest into the launch: our Top 50 = Signal Touch's top-50 high-touch; Next 200 ⊂ its top-500 outbound set; Rest ⊂ its 1,500-account awareness universe.
  • Do once, use twice: the retail research, named case studies, enriched Top 50 / 200 lists, and LinkedIn + WhatsApp (DPDP) setup we build now are exactly what the October launch needs — we're pre-building its assets.
  • Hand-off: Q3's 10 SQLs + a warmed, enriched Top 50 / 200 become the runway into the launch's goal of 100 qualified meetings in 4–6 weeks.
  • Boundary: we do not execute the launch in this plan — we build the engine that makes it land. The launch itself stays in the Signal Touch plan.
