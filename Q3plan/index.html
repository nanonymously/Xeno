What's needed from marketing

From Pranav's review and the Signal Touch org plan, the mandate is clear: marketing exists to create qualified meetings that become SQLs — and to pre-build the October Signal Touch launch. Not vanity reach, not activity for its own sake.

So this quarter is deliberately about conversion over volume: a focused account list, only the activities that have actually produced SQLs, and a clean hand-off to sales. The target is 10 SQLs — a stretch we go all-in on; even if the real win is a system that lands it next quarter, that's success.

2

What we did last year & what worked

Built the engine from zero. A year ago there was no domain, no HubSpot, no infrastructure. Since then: full marketing + GTM / demand-gen ops, all email campaigns, LinkedIn + founder brand, 4 webinars/events, 24 demand-gen assets (excl. Shivalika's), hyper-personalised ABM, multiple website updates, quarter-on-quarter planning, a hire, and ~2,23,000 emails sent. Outcome: 14 SQLs and 5 deals won, marketing-sourced or influenced.

The 82 → 5 drop isn't all failure: some engagements became meetings we didn't log, and many were worked then lost or turned out non-ICP — they just aren't captured. The tracking gap hides the true conversion, which is why fixing it is a Q3 prerequisite (below).

What's actually working (so we double down)

  • Webinars — our highest-intent, highest-quality source.
  • Inbound — best conversion per touch (when we can see where it came from).
  • Interactive content — games + case studies (Squid Games, Loyalty Dilemma) drove our most recent SQLs.
  • Client gifting — planned for Q3 (not run yet); expected to punch above its cost.

Note on the numbers: these are marketing-sourced or influenced — cleaning up that attribution is itself a Q3 commitment, not a claim to hide behind.

3

Current challenges — what's keeping me up at night

1 · Data + tracking gap
Revenue exists on only ~15% of accounts in Clarify — so true target marketing isn't possible yet. And we can't measure engagement → SQL time, or attribute it. Needs enrichment + a real attribution model (already discussed with Fahad & Adrian).
2 · Visibility + accountability
Across marketing → sales → CS there's no shared view of what's handed off, whether it's actioned, and what happens next — and ownership is unclear. We need one visible board and a clear line of accountability.
3 · Budget
Spend decisions have been made without marketing in the loop. I've itemised exactly what's needed and tied every rupee to the plan (see the ask at the end).

The activities

1
Webinars + Events — thought leadership, not product

Webinar (all tiers): purely topical, with industry + customer speakers — a thought-leadership and community play, not a product showcase. This drives more pipeline than a demo webinar, because it earns trust first.

Events (Enterprise): same ethos — a dinner + a topical panel / roundtable, with a customer and ~10 enterprise decision-makers in the room. 3 this quarter: Mumbai, Dubai, Delhi.

Assumed result: ~4 SQLs.

2
Multichannel outreach

Large Filter-level ABM — segmented by job title, department, industry and geo.

Mid Interactive content — case studies + games, run at scale.

Assumed result: ~4 SQLs.

3
Gifting

Enterprise personalised gifting, 12 accounts this quarter (triggered when an account goes "Hot").

Assumed result: enabler — lifts the Enterprise ABM / events conversion (~1 assist).

4
Inbound — tracking + a wider net

Enable source tracking (which leads come from Google vs LinkedIn), then widen the net with low-effort levers: SEO, AEO, LinkedIn posts, backlinking — doubling down only where leads are already coming from.

Assumed result: ~2 SQLs.
